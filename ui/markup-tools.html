VPM-B Physics
Critical radii: each tissue has initial bubble nuclei radii for N₂ and He. Descent compresses (crushes) them. During ascent, allowable supersaturation is derived from bubble mechanics — smaller radii = more conservative stops.
Altitude correction: at altitude, reduced surface pressure expands initial radii by (P_SL / P_alt)^⅓ — making nuclei larger and requiring more conservative ascents. Applied automatically when altitude > 0.
Repetitive dives: bubble nuclei regenerate during surface interval (τ ≈ 14 days). Use the VPM Repetitive mode to carry both tissue loading and bubble state between dives for accurate repetitive dive planning.
