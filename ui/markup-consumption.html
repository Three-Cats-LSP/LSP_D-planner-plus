Practical Diver Notes — MultiDeco FAQ
  • Follow run times. Stop times include transit between stops.
  • No extra deep stops. Adding deep stops significantly increases total obligation.
  • Extending shallow stops (6 m, 3 m) is safe.
  • Ascent rate deviations are most critical in the deep deco zone.
  • Lost deco gas: wait the time difference at current depth, then follow new plan.
  • Conservatism: use +2 or +3 for most divers. +3 or +4 for cold, strenuous, multi-day, or prior DCS history.
  • Do not fake VPM conservatism by inflating depth/time — this breaks the repetitive dive calculations.
  • High O₂ deco mixes after trimix generally do not accelerate deco. Helium deco mixes close to the switch point do accelerate it.
  • First deco gas switch works best when at or just above the first deco stop depth.
Algorithm Comparison
Bühlmann ZH-L16C + GF — dissolved gas model, tunable via GF Low/High. Every major dive computer uses it. Best for any technical dive where you want predictable, well-understood behaviour.
VPM-B — bubble mechanics model (Yount & Hoffman). Tracks microscopic bubble nuclei rather than dissolved gas. Tends to give deeper first stops and shorter shallow stops. Best for divers who prefer a bubble-focused approach.

⚠ Conservatism order is profile-dependent: on deep/long trimix dives GF is usually more conservative (longer TTS); on short/shallow dives VPM-B can match or exceed GF. Compare TTS for your specific dive.
VPM-B+GFS — VPM-B bubble mechanics for deep/mid stops, Bühlmann GF High ceiling at shallow stops. Useful when VPM-B's shallow stops feel too long.
